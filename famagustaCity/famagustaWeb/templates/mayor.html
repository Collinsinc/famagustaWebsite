THE MAYOR OF FAMAGUSTA

İsmail ARTER



Biography

İsmail ARTER was born in 1959 in Larnaca. Arter, who studied primary and secondary school in Larnaka, graduated from Famagusta Namık Kemal High School. Having successfully graduated from Ankara University, Faculty of Agriculture, Department of Agricultural Machinery in 1982, Arter started his master's degree in the same department at Ankara University, Institute of Science with the scholarship of TUBITAK in the same year.
After completing his master's degree in 1984, Arter returned to his country to do his military duty. In 1988, he worked as an Agricultural Engineer, Agricultural Equipment Services and Environmental Protection Department as an Engineer.
During this period, Arter, who served as the Administrative Supervisor at Famagusta State Hospital for more than a year, was appointed as the Prime Ministry Private Secretary in 2003. Elected as a Member of Parliament in the General Elections held on December 14, 2003, Arter served as the Deputy Prime Minister and the Ministry Director and General Coordinator at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs between 2005-2006. Arter, who was a candidate for the Democratic Party Mayor of Famagusta on June 25, 2006, has served as Deputy Prime Minister, Ministry of Economy, Tourism, Culture and Sports, General Coordinator and Chairman of the Board of Trustees of Eastern Mediterranean University since January 27, 2014. Arter, who has a good command of English, is married and has two children.

A city on the east coast of Cyprus. It is located east of Nicosia and possesses the deepest harbour of the island. During the medieval period (especially under the maritime republics of Genoa and Venice), Famagusta was the island's most important port city and a gateway to trade with the ports of the Levant,from where the Silk Road merchants carried their goods to Western Europe.The old walled city and parts of the modern city presently fall within the de facto Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus in Gazimağusa District, of which it is the capital.
