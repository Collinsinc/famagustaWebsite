THE HISTORY OF FAMAGUSTA MUNCIPALITY

Famagusta Turkish Municipality was founded in 1958 for the first time. The first Town Hall was the building in Suriçi, which used to be the police station and nowadays is used as the Department of Antiquities. With the 1960 Constitution, it gained its legal identity with the provisions stipulating that there should be two separate municipalities, Turkish and Greek, in the cities. Until 1974, it served Turkish neighborhoods in Famagusta and a population of approximately 8 - 10 thousand. In the following years, Tuzla Village joined the Municipality, which was reorganized to serve all Famagusta after 1974. The municipality, which started working with a very small number of employees, reached 350 employees in the early 1990s. Famagusta Municipality, which has an area of ​​approximately 93 square kilometers with the Mutluyaka village, which has reached a population of 40 thousand with more than 16 thousand university students and staff in the city for most of the year in addition to 28 thousand citizens living in the city, is the second largest in our country. it is an urban area.

A city on the east coast of Cyprus. It is located east of Nicosia and possesses the deepest harbour of the island. During the medieval period (especially under the maritime republics of Genoa and Venice), Famagusta was the island's most important port city and a gateway to trade with the ports of the Levant,from where the Silk Road merchants carried their goods to Western Europe.The old walled city and parts of the modern city presently fall within the de facto Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus in Gazimağusa District, of which it is the capital.
